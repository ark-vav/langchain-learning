2. **Interoperability**: Components in a sequential chain can be composed from different libraries and frameworks, allowing for a wide range of integration possibilities.
3. **Scalability**: Chains can be extended or modified dynamically by adding new steps or adjusting existing ones, making them adaptable to various use cases.
4. **Flexibility**: Sequential chains offer flexibility in configuration, allowing users to customize the processing pipeline according to specific needs.
5. **Example Workflow**: A typical sequential chain includes steps like input preparation, model execution, output processing, and error handling, illustrated through a text summarization example.
