# **The Gentleman’s Symphony**

The sun awakes with painted fire,
To crown the emerald field,
Where countless hearts, with one desire,
Refuse at last to yield.

A willow rests in calloused hands,
Its grains have stories told,
Of victories sung through distant lands,
And dreams more bright than gold.

The leather sphere, stitched red and proud,
Sleeps silent in the palm,
Before it cuts through cheering crowds,
With fury dressed in calm.

The bowler walks a measured mile,
Each heartbeat marks his pace,
His eyes conceal a cunning smile,
Determined, full of grace.

He gathers wind beneath his feet,
The moment starts to bend,
The crease becomes a battlefield,
Where destinies descend.

The seam spins tales upon the air,
It whispers to the breeze,
It tempts the batter unaware,
With elegant unease.

One leaves, one swings, one curves away,
One crashes into stumps,
Another dances bright as day,
Past slips in joyful jumps.

The batter stands, composed and still,
Like mountains carved by time,
His courage forged by patient will,
His footwork almost rhyme.

A forward press, a gentle glance,
A drive through covers flies,
The crowd erupts as blades of grass
Reflect the open skies.

The pull shot cracks like summer storms,
The hook defies all fear,
The straight drive born in perfect form,
Makes every soul draw near.

The square cut kisses morning light,
The sweep rewrites the game,
The lofted six escapes from sight,
Forever chased by fame.

The keeper crouches low behind,
With gloves like silent wings,
He reads the secrets of the mind,
Before the bowler flings.

A feathered edge, a lightning dive,
A catch with fingertips,
The stadium suddenly comes alive,
With roaring thousand lips.

At slip they stand with patient eyes,
Like hawks that never blink,
Awaiting chances from the skies,
Quicker than one can think.

The spinner walks with ancient art,
No thunder marks his tread,
He conquers not with strength alone,
But puzzles in the head.

His fingers weave invisible threads,
The ball obeys their song,
One drifts away, another spreads,
Then turns where none belong.

The batter lunges far too soon,
Believing all is clear,
The ball returns beneath the moon,
To end his proud career.

The umpire lifts a steady hand,
His finger points above,
The fielders leap across the land,
In celebration's love.

The scoreboard changes every breath,
Each digit tells a tale,
Of hope and pressure, joy and death,
Of triumph, loss, and trail.

Some innings bloom with endless runs,
Like rivers after rain,
While others fade before they've begun,
In sorrow, doubt, and pain.

There comes a child beyond the rope,
Who mimics every star,
His plastic bat contains a hope,
That someday he'll go far.

No stadium lights, no cameras bright,
No trophies line his wall,
Yet every evening feels as right,
Because he plays the ball.

The dusty streets become Lord's ground,
The rooftops guard the fence,
Each broken window bears the sound,
Of youthful confidence.

One slipper marks the batting crease,
A brick becomes the wicket,
Arguments deny all peace,
Yet no one dares to quit it.

"One tip, one hand!" the children cry,
"That ball was surely four!"
The greatest judges standing by,
Are friends forevermore.

From Melbourne's roar to Mumbai's chants,
From Kingston's beating drums,
From Lahore's songs to London's dance,
One language always comes.

It isn't spoken through the tongue,
Nor written into law,
It's carried where the anthem's sung,
In every gasp of awe.

The Test that lasts for five long days,
Rewards the patient soul,
Where every session slowly lays,
Another priceless goal.

The One-Day chase ignites the flame,
With balance, skill, and nerve,
Each over changes all the game,
With courage to preserve.

Then T20 arrives in style,
Exploding like the dawn,
Where every second wears a smile,
Till every chance is gone.

But formats change and years move fast,
The spirit stays the same,
Respect for rivals always lasts,
Beyond the final game.

The captain bears a heavy crown,
Not forged from gems or gold,
But every choice may lift or drown,
The dreams his teammates hold.

A handshake after bitter loss,
A smile despite the tears,
Reminds us victories have a cost,
Yet character endures the years.

For cricket is not bat and ball,
Nor records set in stone,
It teaches one to rise from fall,
And stand though all alone.

It teaches patience through the wait,
And courage through the pain,
That every setback shapes our fate,
Before the joy we gain.

It whispers softly to the weak,
"Your finest hour may come."
It tells the proud to always seek,
Humility in every run.

And when the final light grows dim,
The crowd begins to part,
The echoes form a timeless hymn,
Still beating in each heart.

The pitch grows quiet, shadows fall,
The cheers become the breeze,
Yet somewhere, someone grips a ball,
Beneath familiar trees.

Another dream prepares to rise,
Another tale takes flight,
Another child with hopeful eyes,
Awaits tomorrow's light.

For cricket is more than a game to win,
More than statistics, score, or fame;
It is a story written within,
A fire that every heart can claim.

So let the willow sing once more,
Let leather kiss the sky,
For while a single soul keeps score,
The game will never die.
